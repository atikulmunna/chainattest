# Contributions And Thesis Framing

## Thesis

ChainAttest is a research prototype for moving machine-learning attestations across chains as typed evidence rather than as assets or opaque messages.

## Core Contributions

1. Typed ML attestation packages rather than asset-transfer payloads.
2. ZK verification over ML-specific commitments and evaluation claims.
3. Privacy-preserving evaluation threshold proofs bound to structured transcript summaries.
4. Committee-authenticated cross-chain source records combined with destination-side semantic verification.
5. A compliance-evidence framing for public AI provenance and traceability records.

## Prototype Claim

The current prototype demonstrates a credible EVM-to-EVM destination-verification stack with reproducible orchestration, signer isolation, and local demo automation.
